CCA

  I was in Sea Scouts for all 4 years of my time in secondary school. Initially nudged into their CCA trials by my classmate in secondary 1, I was tricked by the scout seniors and begrudgingly joined their forces.
  For the first 3 years, I've hated everything about the CCA. From its uncomfortable uniform to their unfair punishments. But slowly as the months and years went by, I started to slowly appriciate some of the programs planned for us.

Campfire

  It started with our campfire sessions, where for one semester, every scouts lesson was just us learning how to set up a campfire, and my job was to pick up twigs and sticks to both start and maintain the fire. I loved this job, as I was able to spend my time together with my good friends just hanging out and collecting sticks while we talked about our lives, as the days go by, more and more people joined me picking sticks, and I became good friends with many of my peers. I started to respect Scouts a little bit after the campfire program ended, thinking back to the amazing experiences picking up twigs of all things.

Overall

  Overall, I would say that I had a pretty decent time in Scouts. I met a bunch of very nice people, forged many otherwise impossible friendships, had a ton of fun in many of our scouts activities.
  Although I had a fair share of hardships during my time there, I overall enjoyed my time there, though if I could live through my secondary school life again, I would probably choose another CCA, just because scout was not my first choice back in secondary 1.
