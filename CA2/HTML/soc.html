What I'm learning

  As of writing this blog, I am currently a year 1 student, and my modules can be split into 2 parts: Core modules, and general moudules. Starting off with the core modules, there are Fundementals of Commputing(FoC), Fundementals of Programming(FoP), and Front-End Web Development(FED).

  In FoC, we learn how do computers work, not just on the surface level, but also deeper down like how the CPU works or how the internet works, I personally do not really enjoy this module as my lecturer is really hard to understand, and he speaks really quickly, so catching up to his speed is an uphill task, and now I've resorted to just self-studying the course notes.

  In FoP, we learn about how to program in a language know as JavaScript. In this course, we need to constantly turn our brains to think of how to program the solutions to our problems at hand, and is my personal favourite module of the bunch, as the feeling of satisfaction you get after coompleting a hard problem is really amazing, and this course has also taught me to be more observant to find out issues with my code, so I would say that is nice added benefit.

  In FED, we learn how to create websites, like the one that you are on right now. Everything from HTML to add contents to the website, to CSS to style the website, and JavaScript for interactiive features, we learn all the basics to create a functional website. This is not my favourite course, mainly beasue it requires some creative elements when creating websites, which is not my strong suit.

And here is a summary of my non-core modules. These modules for the most part are not unique to my course, and many students from other classes are likely taking the same or similiar modules to us. These modules are: Math, Communicating for PRoject effectiveness(CPR), Critical and Analytical Thinking(CAT), Mathematics, and Education and Career Guidance(ECG).

  Starting off with CPR, this course pits you with 3-4 of your classmates to come up with a solution to a real world problem, and then forming a proposal and doing a presentation on our solution. I personally do not mind this course as our lecturer is a nice person, and I find it rather fun to work with other people that I don't know as it increases my contacts as well as significantly lightens the workload of an individual. Overall, I quite enjoy this course.

  Next up is CAT. Similiarly to the aformentioned CPR, this module pits us with 3-4 of your classmates with similiar interests and hobbies. In this course, we learn how to check validity of sources when doing research on a topic. Our assignment in this module was to come up with a question that would hold some significance in other people's lives, in my case, my team went with "Do video games cause violence?" as our group's common interest was video games. Overall, my experience with CAT has been rather dull, as my group rarely communicates with each other, and we do almost everything either last minute or straight up late.

  Now onto Mathmatics, this course was actually specially curated for video game creation in mind so it is in some ways a core module but I'll consider it as a non-core module for this blog. As previously mentioned, this course is specially designed for video game creators, learning about math concepts such as probability that should help us when greating games. My lecturer for this module is rather friendly, approachable and flexible, and thus I actually really enjoy my time in his lessons very much. Overall, very enjoyable course.
